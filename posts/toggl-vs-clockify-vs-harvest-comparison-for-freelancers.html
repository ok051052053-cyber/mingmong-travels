Toggl vs Clockify vs Harvest: The Ultimate Time Tracking Comparison for Freelancers

Productivity • Updated: 2026-03-04

Introduction

In the fast-paced world of freelancing, time tracking is essential. It not only helps in managing workloads but also in invoicing clients accurately. Among various time-tracking tools available, Toggl, Clockify, and Harvest stand out. This article offers a comprehensive comparison to help freelancers choose the best tool for their needs.

1. Overview of the Three Tools

Each of these tools has unique features, pricing models, and usability aspects. Let’s take a brief look at each:

  • Toggl: Known for its simplicity and user-friendly interface, Toggl is ideal for freelancers who prioritize ease of use. It also offers robust reporting features.
  • Clockify: A completely free option that boasts impressive features, making it popular among budget-conscious freelancers. Clockify allows unlimited tracking and users.
  • Harvest: A more feature-rich option, Harvest integrates with various project management tools, making it suitable for freelancers managing multiple projects at once.

2. Feature Comparison

Understanding features is crucial for freelancers who need specific functionalities. Here’s how Toggl, Clockify, and Harvest stack up:

2.1 Time Tracking

  • Toggl: Offers manual and automatic time tracking. The mobile and desktop apps are intuitive, allowing users to easily start and stop timers.
  • Clockify: Similar to Toggl in functionality, Clockify’s free version provides all essential time tracking features, including timer and manual entry options.
  • Harvest: Provides both manual and auto-tracking options. It also includes a timesheet feature that can simplify billable hours tracking.

2.2 Reporting

  • Toggl: Features advanced reporting tools that help freelancers analyze where their time goes. Reports can be exported easily for client presentations.
  • Clockify: Offers basic reporting that is effective but less extensive compared to Toggl and Harvest. Customization options are limited.
  • Harvest: Known for excellent reporting capabilities, it allows visual data representation, making it easy for freelancers to understand their time usage.

2.3 Integrations

  • Toggl: Integrates with various tools like Asana, Trello, and Slack, enhancing productivity by connecting your workflows.
  • Clockify: Offers integration with a range of tools like Google Calendar and Jira, although fewer compared to Toggl and Harvest.
  • Harvest: Excels in integrations, supporting over 100 third-party applications, making it highly flexible for complex workflows.

3. Pricing Structure

Understanding pricing can significantly impact a freelancer’s decision. Here’s a quick overview:

  • Toggl: Free tier available, but premium plans start at $10 per user/month, which includes advanced features such as project tracking.
  • Clockify: Free forever for all users, with premium features available starting at $3.99 per user/month, including larger teams and advanced reporting.
  • Harvest: Offers a free plan for up to two projects. Paid plans begin at $12 per month per user, providing more features and functionalities.

4. Usability

User experience can make or break a tool, especially for freelancers juggling multiple clients:

  • Toggl: Highly praised for its clean interface, Toggl is easy to navigate, even for those who are not tech-savvy.
  • Clockify: Also user-friendly, though some users may find it slightly less intuitive than Toggl.
  • Harvest: While powerful, it can be overwhelming due to the number of features available, which might require a learning curve.

5. Pros and Cons

Here’s a summary of the strengths and weaknesses of each tool:

5.1 Toggl

  • Pros: Simple interface, robust reporting, great integrations.
  • Cons: Higher pricing for premium features.

5.2 Clockify

  • Pros: Completely free, unlimited tracking and users.
  • Cons: Basic reporting capabilities.

5.3 Harvest

  • Pros: Extensive integrations and good reporting tools.
  • Cons: Limited free option and potential complexity for new users.

Conclusion

Choosing the right time-tracking tool ultimately depends on your specific needs as a freelancer. If simplicity and advanced reporting are critical, Toggl is a solid choice. For those on a budget, Clockify offers valuable features at no cost. Harvest suits freelancers needing extensive integrations and advanced functionalities.

Evaluate your priorities and even try out these tools through their free trials to find the best fit for your freelancing journey!
