How to Price Freelance Copywriting in the US

Make Money • Updated: 2026-03-03

Understanding Your Value

As a freelance copywriter, setting your rates can be challenging. It’s essential to reflect on your experience, skills, and the value you bring to clients. Here are some key steps to determine your pricing:

1. Research Market Rates

Start by researching what other freelance copywriters charge. Consider the following:

  • Industry averages: Websites like Glassdoor and PayScale can provide insights.
  • Networking: Join freelance communities on platforms like LinkedIn and Reddit.
  • Competitors: Look at portfolios of similar freelancers on Upwork and Fiverr.

2. Consider Your Experience

Your level of expertise greatly impacts your pricing:

  • Beginner: $0.10 to $0.25 per word or $25 to $50 per hour.
  • Intermediate: $0.25 to $0.50 per word or $50 to $100 per hour.
  • Expert: $0.50 to $1.00+ per word or $100+ per hour.

3. Choose Your Pricing Model

Decide which pricing model works best for you:

  • Per Word: Commonly used for blog posts and articles.
  • Hourly Rate: Suitable for projects with unclear scope.
  • Project-Based: Ideal for specific tasks like website copy or brochures.

4. Evaluate the Project Scope

Before quoting a price, assess the following:

  • Length of the content
  • Research required
  • Client’s urgency
  • Revision policies

5. Factor in Your Unique Selling Proposition

If you specialize in a niche (like health, finance, or technology), you can charge higher rates due to your expertise. Make sure to communicate this value to potential clients.

6. Set Minimum Rates

Establish a floor rate to ensure that you are compensated fairly for your time and effort. This will help you avoid underpricing your services.

7. Reevaluate Regularly

As you gain experience, continue to reassess your rates. Consider raising your prices based on:

  • Increased demand for your services
  • Expanded portfolio and testimonials
  • Additional skills or certifications

Conclusion

Pricing freelance copywriting can feel daunting, but with research, self-awareness, and a clear understanding of your value, you can set rates that reflect your expertise. Remember to communicate effectively with clients and remain flexible as you grow in your freelance career.
